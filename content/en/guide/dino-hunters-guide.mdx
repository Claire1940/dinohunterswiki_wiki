<Section icon={Swords} title="Weapon Upgrades and Progression Path">

Your survival depends entirely on the quality of your gear. Weapon progression in Dino Hunters is linear but requires significant resource investment. Upgrading a weapon doesn't just increase its damage; it often unlocks secondary fire modes or specialized ammo slots that are vital for taking down armored dinosaurs.

The upgrade process involves gathering specific ores and monster parts. For example, upgrading a standard rifle to a "Hardened Steel Rifle" requires Iron Ore and Raptor Claws. Always check the upgrade bench in the main hub to see which materials you need to farm next. This targeted farming approach prevents wasted time and ensures you are always at the appropriate power level for your current zone.

<Section icon={Target} title="Advanced Hunting Tactics and Boss Mechanics">

Once you have mastered the basics, the real challenge begins with Boss Dinosaurs. These massive creatures have unique mechanics that require more than just raw firepower. To succeed, you must learn to read their attack patterns and exploit their specific weaknesses. Most bosses have "telegraphed" attacks—visual cues that indicate a powerful move is coming.

For instance, the Elder Rex will roar and stomp its feet before performing a massive area-of-effect (AoE) tail swipe. Seeing this cue allows you to dodge backward and avoid lethal damage. Combining your dinosaur team's abilities with your own weapon fire is the most efficient way to bring these giants down. Use a tanky dinosaur like the Triceratops to draw aggro while you deal damage from a safe distance with a sniper rifle.
