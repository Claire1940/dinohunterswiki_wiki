<Section icon={Target} title="Systematic Excavation: From Discovery to Market">

Successfully navigating the **dino hunters updates** requires a disciplined approach to the excavation process. As seen in the Season 2 promo, finding the bone is only the beginning. The transition from "bone on top of bone" to a marketable museum piece involves several high-stakes steps that require both brute force and extreme delicacy.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Prospecting and Initial Strike">
    Identify surface fragments (float) and trace them back to the source layer. Once a "bone on top of bone" site is found, clear the overburden with heavy machinery.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Delicate Uncovering">
    Switch from excavators to hand tools. Use awls, brushes, and dental picks to reveal the fossil's orientation without causing fractures to the prehistoric material.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Field Jacketing">
    Apply burlap and plaster of Paris to create a rigid "jacket" around the fossil and its surrounding matrix. This protects the specimen during the grueling transport process.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Extraction and Transport">
    Use cranes or heavy-duty winches to lift the jacketed fossil. This is the most dangerous phase, as the weight can reach several tons and equipment failure is catastrophic.
  </Step>
</Steps>

| Tool Type | Primary Use | Precision Level | Required Skill |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Excavator** | Overburden removal | Low | Heavy Equipment Op |
| **Pneumatic Chisel** | Matrix removal | Medium | Field Technician |
| **Fine Brushes** | Surface cleaning | **High** | Paleontologist |
| **Plaster/Burlap** | Stabilization | Medium | General Crew |

<Callout type="tip" title="Pro Tip">
Always leave a "pedestal" of stone beneath the fossil during the jacketing process to ensure the specimen has structural support when it is flipped for transport.
</Callout>

**Valuation Factors:**
- **Completeness**: The percentage of the original skeleton recovered.
- **Articulation**: Whether the bones are found in their original anatomical positions.
- **Pathology**: Evidence of injuries or diseases on the bones, which adds scientific value.
- **Preparation Quality**: How well the fossil was cleaned and restored in the lab.
