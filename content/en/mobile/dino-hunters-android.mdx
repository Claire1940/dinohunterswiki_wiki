<Section icon={Target} title="Core Gameplay Mechanics & Mission Objectives">

The primary loop of the game revolves around hunting predatory dinosaurs and rescuing survivors scattered across various zones. Each mission presents unique challenges, ranging from static defense points to high-speed vehicle pursuits where accuracy is tested under pressure.

Rescuing civilians requires quick targeting. Dinosaurs will actively target hostages, and a countdown timer essentially runs in the form of the survivor's health bar. Prioritizing targets based on their proximity to the survivors is crucial for securing a perfect mission rating.

| Mission Type | Primary Objective | Target Priority |
|---|---|---|
| Hunt | Eliminate all target dinosaurs | High-threat predators |
| Rescue | Save civilians or children | Dinosaurs nearest to hostages |
| Survival | Outlast waves of predators | Fast-moving melee attackers |
| Pursuit | Chase targets using vehicles | Obstacles and pursuing dinosaurs |

Critical hits are the fastest way to neutralize targets. Headshots deal multiplied damage and can stagger dinosaurs, stopping their advance temporarily. Utilizing the slow-motion button during a charge gives you the window needed to align your crosshairs with the target's weak points.

<Callout type="tip" title="Target Priority">
When multiple dinosaurs are on the screen, always eliminate the ones closest to civilians first. Even if a larger dinosaur is nearby, a single raptor can quickly end a rescue mission if left unchecked.
</Callout>

| Weapon Class | Optimal Range | Damage Potential | Handling |
|---|---|---|---|

When engaging large targets like the Tyrannosaurus Rex, rely on heavy sniper rifles to deal massive damage from afar. If the target charges, swap to an assault rifle or shotgun to fire rapid shots into its weak points while moving backward to maintain distance.
