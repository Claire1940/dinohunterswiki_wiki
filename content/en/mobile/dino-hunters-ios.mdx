<Section icon={Target} title="The Core Hunting Loop: Step-by-Step">

Mastering the hunt in **Dino Hunters iOS** requires more than just firepower; it requires patience and tactical awareness. New players often make the mistake of rushing into high-level zones without proper preparation. Following a structured approach to your first few hours will ensure you don't waste precious resources on failed captures.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Equip Your Starter Loadout">
    Begin by visiting the Hunter's Hub. Select the basic **Tranq-Rifle** and a set of standard Dino-Nets. These tools are essential for capturing your first mount, which will significantly increase your exploration speed across the map.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Track Your Prey">
    Use the "Tracker" tool located on the right side of the iOS interface. This will highlight footprints and disturbed foliage. Follow these markers until you locate a Common-tier dinosaur, such as a Parasaurolophus or a small Raptor.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Engage and Weaken">
    Lower the dinosaur's health to below 25% using your primary weapon. Be careful not to deplete its health entirely, or you will lose the opportunity to capture it. On iOS, use the "Tap-to-Lock" feature to ensure your shots land consistently.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Deploy the Capture Net">
    Once the dinosaur is weakened, switch to your Capture Net. A mini-game will appear on your screen—simply follow the directional swipes to secure the capture. Successful captures grant you the dinosaur as a permanent pet and mount.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning" title="Stamina Management">
Running and jumping in Dino Hunters iOS consumes stamina quickly. If you run out of stamina while being chased by a T-Rex, you will likely be sent back to the nearest respawn point. Always keep a few "Stamina Berries" in your quick-slot.
</Callout>

</Section>
